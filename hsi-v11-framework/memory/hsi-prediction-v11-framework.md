## ⚠️ Important Distinction: Solar Terms vs. High-Confidence Windows

A common source of confusion is the difference between **Solar Term Key Dates** and **Gann High-Confidence Windows**. They are calculated differently and serve different purposes:

### Solar Term Key Dates (Astronomical)
| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **What** | Fixed dates when sun reaches specific ecliptic longitudes |
| **Purpose** | Seasonal energy filter - tells you **WHEN** market psychology shifts |
| **Example** | 立春 (Feb 4), 春分 (Mar 20), 冬至 (Dec 21) |
| **Usage** | "Be bullish in spring, accumulate in winter" |
| **Variability** | Fixed calendar dates (same every year, ±1 day) |

### Gann High-Confidence Windows (Technical)
| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **What** | Calculated dates where Gann time/price factors converge |
| **Purpose** | Quantitative trading signals - tells you **WHERE** to trade heavily |
| **Example** | Oct 6-10 (90 pts), May 9 (70 pts), Dec 21-26 (75 pts) |
| **Usage** | "Trade heavily on Oct 6-10 (90 pts confluence)" |
| **Variability** | Calculated per model run, based on historical pivots + geometry |

### When They Align = Maximum Opportunity 🔥

| Date | Solar Term | Gann Confluence | Combined Signal |
|------|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Dec 21-26 | 冬至 (Winter Solstice) | 75 pts | 🔥 Maximum accumulation zone |
| Jun 21-25 | 夏至 (Summer Solstice) | 75 pts | 🔥 Peak/reversal warning |
| Aug 7-12 | 立秋 (Start of Autumn) | 65 pts | 🟡 Harvest/profit-taking |

**Key Insight**: Solar terms provide the **seasonal backdrop**, Gann confluence provides the **specific trigger dates**. Best trades occur when both align.

### Why May 9 (70 pts) Isn't a Solar Term

The May 9 high-confidence window comes from:
- Gann 120° triangle completion from 2004 peak
- Historical pivot anniversary patterns
- Price level confluence at key Fibonacci retracement

It's a **purely technical Gann calculation**, not tied to any solar term. This is why it appears in the High-Confidence Windows list but not in the Solar Term calendar.
