# 蔡森 (Cai Sen) Volume-Price Analysis Methodology

## Core Principles

蔡森's trading methodology is built on four fundamental principles:

1. **量在價先 (Volume Precedes Price)** - Volume changes always come before price movements. Watch volume first.
2. **小賠大賺 (Small Losses, Big Gains)** - Cut losses quickly, let profits run. Risk/reeward ratio must favor gains.
3. **嚴守停損 (Strict Stop-Loss)** - Never violate your stop-loss. Survival is more important than being right.
4. **隨勢而為 (Follow the Trend)** - Trade with the trend, not against it. The trend is your friend.

## The 12 Patterns

### 1. W-bottom (雙重底)

**Visual Description:**
- Price forms two distinct lows at approximately the same level
- Looks like the letter "W" on the chart
- Second bottom may be slightly higher than the first (bullish sign)
- Neckline resistance connects the peak between the two bottoms

**Volume Characteristics:**
- First bottom: Heavy volume (panic selling)
- Rally to neckline: Moderate volume
- Second bottom: Lighter volume than first (selling exhaustion)
- Breakout above neckline: **Heavy volume** (critical confirmation)

**Entry Signal:**
- Buy when price breaks above neckline with volume confirmation
- Alternative: Buy on pullback to neckline after breakout (if it holds as support)

**Stop Loss:**
- Place below the second bottom (or below the lower of the two bottoms)
- Typical distance: 5-8% below entry

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the height from bottom to neckline
- Project same distance upward from neckline breakout point
- Example: Bottom at 100, neckline at 120 → Target = 120 + (120-100) = 140

**Success Rate:**
- ~70-75% when volume confirms breakout
- Higher success in oversold markets or after extended downtrends

**Example:**
Stock falls from 150 to 100 on heavy volume, bounces to 120, falls back to 102 on lighter volume, then breaks above 120 on surge in volume. Target: 140.

### 2. 破底翻 (Spring / False Breakdown)

**Visual Description:**
- Price breaks below established support level
- Quickly reverses and closes back above support
- Looks like a "bear trap" - breaks down then immediately springs back up
- Often occurs at the bottom of a trading range

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Breakdown: May have moderate volume (looks like real breakdown)
- Reversal day: **Very heavy volume** (smart money buying)
- Follow-through: Continued above-average volume

**Entry Signal:**
- Buy when price closes back above support level
- More aggressive: Buy on the reversal day itself if volume is exceptional

**Stop Loss:**
- Place below the low of the spring (the false breakdown low)
- Typical distance: 3-5% below entry

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the trading range height
- Project upward from breakout point
- Or use previous resistance level as target

**Success Rate:**
- ~75-80% (one of the highest probability patterns)
- Especially powerful after long consolidation periods

**Example:**
Stock trades between 50-60 for months. Suddenly drops to 47 on moderate volume, then surges to close at 58 on massive volume. This is institutional accumulation.

---

### 3. M-top (雙重頂)

**Visual Description:**
- Price forms two distinct peaks at approximately the same level
- Looks like the letter "M" on the chart
- Second peak may be slightly lower than the first (bearish sign)
- Neckline support connects the trough between the two peaks

**Volume Characteristics:**
- First peak: Heavy volume
- Decline to neckline: Moderate volume
- Second peak: **Lighter volume** than first (buying exhaustion - divergence!)
- Breakdown below neckline: Volume may increase

**Entry Signal:**
- Sell/short when price breaks below neckline
- Can also sell at second peak if volume divergence is clear

**Stop Loss:**
- Place above the higher of the two peaks
- Typical distance: 5-8% above entry

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the height from peak to neckline
- Project same distance downward from neckline breakdown
- Example: Peak at 200, neckline at 180 → Target = 180 - (200-180) = 160

**Success Rate:**
- ~70% when volume divergence is present
- Higher success after extended uptrends

**Example:**
Stock rallies from 100 to 200 on heavy volume, pulls back to 180, rallies to 198 on lighter volume, then breaks below 180. Target: 160.

---

### 4. 假突破 (False Breakout)

**Visual Description:**
- Price breaks above resistance (or below support)
- Fails to follow through
- Reverses back into the trading range
- Looks like a "bull trap" (at tops) or "bear trap" (at bottoms)

**Volume Characteristics:**
- **At Tops (Bearish):**
  - Breakout: Low or moderate volume (lack of conviction)
  - Reversal: Heavy volume (smart money selling)
  
- **At Bottoms (Bullish):**
  - Breakdown: Low volume (lack of selling pressure)
  - Reversal: Heavy volume (smart money buying)

**Entry Signal:**
- Wait for the trap to spring - enter when price reverses back into range
- For bearish false breakout: Sell when price falls back below resistance
- For bullish false breakout: Buy when price rises back above support

**Stop Loss:**
- Place beyond the false breakout extreme
- Typical distance: 3-5%

**Target Calculation:**
- Target the opposite side of the trading range
- Or use measured move based on range height

**Success Rate:**
- ~70-75%
- Very common at major market tops and bottoms

**Example:**
Stock resistance at 100. Breaks to 103 on light volume, then crashes to 95 on heavy volume next day. Classic bull trap.

### 5. 頭肩頂 (Head & Shoulders Top)

**Visual Description:**
- Three peaks: left shoulder, head (highest), right shoulder
- Head is clearly higher than both shoulders
- Neckline connects the lows between shoulders and head
- Right shoulder often has lower volume than left

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Left shoulder: Heavy volume
- Head: Moderate volume (often less than left shoulder)
- Right shoulder: **Light volume** (clear divergence)
- Neckline breakdown: Volume increases

**Entry Signal:**
- Sell when price breaks below neckline
- Aggressive: Sell at right shoulder if volume divergence is extreme

**Stop Loss:**
- Place above the head (or above right shoulder for tighter stop)
- Typical distance: 5-10%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure from head top to neckline
- Project downward from neckline breakout
- Example: Head at 250, neckline at 220 → Target = 220 - (250-220) = 190

**Success Rate:**
- ~75% (one of the most reliable reversal patterns)
- Higher success when pattern develops over longer time period

**Example:**
Stock: Left shoulder 200, head 230, right shoulder 205. Neckline at 190. Break below 190 → Target 160.

---

### 6. 頭肩底 (Head & Shoulders Bottom / Inverse H&S)

**Visual Description:**
- Three troughs: left shoulder, head (lowest), right shoulder
- Head is clearly lower than both shoulders
- Neckline connects the highs between shoulders and head
- Mirror image of H&S top

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Left shoulder: Heavy volume (selling)
- Head: Moderate volume (less selling pressure)
- Right shoulder: **Light volume** (selling exhausted)
- Neckline breakout: **Very heavy volume** (critical!)

**Entry Signal:**
- Buy when price breaks above neckline with volume
- Conservative: Wait for pullback to neckline after breakout

**Stop Loss:**
- Place below the head (or below right shoulder for tighter stop)
- Typical distance: 5-8%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure from head bottom to neckline
- Project upward from neckline breakout
- Example: Head at 80, neckline at 100 → Target = 100 + (100-80) = 120

**Success Rate:**
- ~75-80% with volume confirmation
- Very reliable bottom reversal pattern

**Example:**
Stock: Left shoulder 100, head 85, right shoulder 95. Neckline at 105. Break above 105 on heavy volume → Target 125.

---

### 7. 收斂三角形頭部 (Descending Triangle / 下降三角形)

**Visual Description:**
- Flat support line at the bottom
- Descending resistance line (lower highs)
- Price converges toward apex
- Typically bearish continuation pattern

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Volume typically **decreases** as pattern forms
- Breakdown below support: Volume **increases**
- Failed upside bounces: Light volume

**Entry Signal:**
- Sell when price breaks below flat support
- Wait for volume confirmation on breakdown

**Stop Loss:**
- Place above the most recent lower high
- Or above the triangle's upper trendline
- Typical distance: 5-7%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the height of the triangle at its widest point
- Project downward from breakdown point
- Example: Triangle height 20 points, support at 100 → Target = 100 - 20 = 80

**Success Rate:**
- ~65-70%
- Works best as continuation pattern in downtrend

**Example:**
Stock in downtrend. Forms triangle with support at 100, resistance declining from 115 to 105. Breaks 100 on volume → Target 85.

---

### 8. 收斂三角形底部 (Ascending Triangle / 上升三角形)

**Visual Description:**
- Flat resistance line at the top
- Ascending support line (higher lows)
- Price converges toward apex
- Typically bullish continuation pattern

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Volume typically **decreases** as pattern forms
- Breakout above resistance: Volume **surges**
- Pullbacks to support: Light volume

**Entry Signal:**
- Buy when price breaks above flat resistance with volume
- Conservative: Wait for retest of resistance as support

**Stop Loss:**
- Place below the most recent higher low
- Or below the triangle's lower trendline
- Typical distance: 5-7%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the height of the triangle at its widest point
- Project upward from breakout point
- Example: Triangle height 15 points, resistance at 50 → Target = 50 + 15 = 65

**Success Rate:**
- ~70-75%
- Works best as continuation pattern in uptrend

**Example:**
Stock in uptrend. Forms triangle with resistance at 50, support rising from 40 to 47. Breaks 50 on heavy volume → Target 60.

---

### 9. 上飄旗形 (Bearish Flag)

**Visual Description:**
- Sharp decline (flagpole) on heavy volume
- Followed by upward-sloping consolidation (the flag)
- Flag slopes against the main trend (upward in downtrend)
- Looks like a flag on a pole

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Flagpole (initial decline): **Heavy volume**
- Flag (consolidation): **Light, decreasing volume**
- Breakdown from flag: Volume increases again

**Entry Signal:**
- Sell when price breaks below lower flag boundary
- Enter on breakdown with volume confirmation

**Stop Loss:**
- Place above the top of the flag
- Typical distance: 3-5%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the flagpole length
- Project same distance from breakout point
- Example: Flagpole from 100 to 80 (20 points), flag breaks at 85 → Target = 85 - 20 = 65

**Success Rate:**
- ~70-75%
- Very reliable continuation pattern

**Example:**
Stock crashes from 200 to 170 on heavy volume, then drifts up to 180 on light volume over 2 weeks. Breaks below 170 → Target 140.

---

### 10. 下飄旗形 (Bullish Flag)

**Visual Description:**
- Sharp rally (flagpole) on heavy volume
- Followed by downward-sloping consolidation (the flag)
- Flag slopes against the main trend (downward in uptrend)
- Mirror image of bearish flag

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Flagpole (initial rally): **Heavy volume**
- Flag (consolidation): **Light, decreasing volume**
- Breakout from flag: Volume surges

**Entry Signal:**
- Buy when price breaks above upper flag boundary
- Enter on breakout with volume confirmation

**Stop Loss:**
- Place below the bottom of the flag
- Typical distance: 3-5%

**Target Calculation:**
- Measure the flagpole length
- Project same distance from breakout point
- Example: Flagpole from 50 to 70 (20 points), flag breaks at 68 → Target = 68 + 20 = 88

**Success Rate:**
- ~75% (one of the most reliable patterns)
- Excellent risk/reward ratio

**Example:**
Stock surges from 100 to 130 on massive volume, then drifts down to 120 on light volume. Breaks above 125 → Target 155.

### 11. 問題量 (Problem Volume / Volume Divergence)

**Visual Description:**
- Not a specific chart pattern, but a warning signal
- Price makes new high but volume is lower than previous high
- Or price rises on declining volume (divergence)
- Often precedes reversals

**Volume Characteristics:**
- **Bearish Divergence:** Price ↑, Volume ↓
- **Bullish Divergence:** Price ↓, Volume ↓ (selling exhaustion)
- **Problem Volume:** Price makes new extreme on suspiciously low volume

**Entry Signal:**
- This is an EXIT signal, not an entry signal
- When you see divergence in your favor, consider taking profits
- When you see divergence against your position, prepare to exit

**Stop Loss:**
- Not applicable - this is a warning signal
- Use to tighten existing stops or take partial profits

**Target Calculation:**
- Not applicable
- Use to manage existing positions

**Success Rate:**
- ~65-70% as a warning signal
- Not reliable as standalone trading signal
- Must be combined with other patterns

**Example:**
Stock at 100 on volume 10M. Rallies to 110 on volume 7M. Rallies to 115 on volume 5M. Clear divergence - smart money is not participating. Reversal likely.

---

### 12. 時間波 (Time Waves / Time Cycles)

**Visual Description:**
- Not a visual pattern but a timing concept
- Markets move in cyclical time patterns
- Important turning points often occur at regular intervals
- Based on previous significant highs/lows

**Volume Characteristics:**
- Volume often increases at time cycle turning points
- Watch for volume confirmation when time target is reached
- Low volume at time target suggests weak move

**Entry Signal:**
- Use time cycles to anticipate potential turning points
- Wait for price/volume confirmation before entering
- Combine with other patterns for higher probability

**Stop Loss:**
- Standard stops based on pattern or support/resistance
- Time cycles don't provide stop levels

**Target Calculation:**
- Project forward from significant highs/lows
- Common cycles: 30, 60, 90, 180, 360 days
- Also watch weekly/monthly anniversaries

**Success Rate:**
- ~60-65% standalone
- Much higher (~75%) when combined with other patterns
- Best used as timing filter, not primary signal

**Example:**
Stock made major low on Jan 15. Watch for potential turn around Apr 15 (90 days), Jul 15 (180 days), or next Jan 15 (360 days). If W-bottom also forms at time target, high probability trade.

## Pattern Priority & Combination

### Highest Probability Setups

1. **Spring (破底翻) + Time Cycle** - 80%+ success
2. **Bullish Flag (下飄旗形) in uptrend** - 75% success
3. **Head & Shoulders Bottom + Volume confirmation** - 75-80% success
4. **W-bottom with volume surge** - 70-75% success
5. **Ascending Triangle in uptrend** - 70-75% success

### Pattern Combinations

- **W-bottom + Problem Volume divergence at second top** = Early warning
- **Head & Shoulders + Time Cycle target** = High probability reversal
- **False Breakout + Spring** = Very powerful reversal signal
- **Flag pattern + Volume drying up** = Continuation likely

## Risk Management Rules

1. **Never risk more than 2% of capital on any single trade**
2. **Always use stop-loss orders** - no exceptions
3. **Minimum risk/reward ratio: 1:2** (target should be 2x the risk)
4. **Scale out of winners** - take partial profits at targets
5. **Never add to losing positions** - only add to winners

## Volume Analysis Quick Reference

| Volume Pattern | Price Action | Interpretation |
|---------------|--------------|----------------|
| Heavy ↑ | Up ↑ | Bullish confirmation |
| Heavy ↑ | Down ↓ | Bearish confirmation |
| Light ↓ | Up ↑ | Weak rally (divergence) |
| Light ↓ | Down ↓ | Selling exhaustion |
| Surge | Breakout | Valid breakout |
| Light | Breakout | False breakout likely |
| Heavy | At support | Accumulation |
| Heavy | At resistance | Distribution |
